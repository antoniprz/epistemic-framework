# C3 Diagnostic: The Private Language Bridge

## 1. The Problem: Wittgenstein’s Bottleneck
Ludwig Wittgenstein struggled with the gap between private sensation and public language. 
* In his early work (**Tractatus**), he argued that if we cannot logically map a sensation, we must remain silent. 
* In his later work (**Investigations**), he argued that meaning is just a "language game" defined by community consensus.

**The Framework's Solution:** We introduce **C3** as an isolated layer for pre-linguistic subjective experience. It is the bridge that prevents the "collapse" of private feelings into absolute physical laws.

## 2. The Fallacy of Layer Attribution
This is a specific semantic bug where an arguer attempts to bypass the C3 bridge to claim that a C5 convention (social code) is a C1 reality (physical law).

### Case Study: Moral Dumbfounding (Jonathan Haidt)
**Scenario:** A family decides to eat their pet dog after it was accidentally killed by a car. They cook it privately and no one else is harmed.
**The Reaction:** Most people feel a visceral sense of disgust.
**The Logic Error:**
1. **C3 (Sensation):** "I feel a visceral disgust." (Internal, pre-linguistic).
2. **C4/C5 (Translation):** The person scrambles to find a reason: "It's harmful!" or "It's objectively evil!"
3. **The Bug:** Since there is no **C1 harm** (the dog was already dead), the person is falsely attributing their **C3 disgust** to a **C1 physical violation**.

## 3. Diagnostic Checklist (The Audit)
Use this checklist when an argument claims a "universal truth" based on a value judgment or moral stance.

- [ ] **Step 1: Trace to C1/C2.** Is there a measurable physical harm, a transfer of energy, or a biological fact involved? (e.g., broken bones, stolen money, pathogens).
- [ ] **Step 2: Isolate the C3 Reaction.** Does the arguer experience a visceral sensation (disgust, fear, pleasure) *before* they start explaining why?
- [ ] **Step 3: Check for Layer Bypass.** Is the arguer using the C3 sensation as the *sole* proof for a C1/C5 claim? 
    * *Failure:* "This is wrong because it feels wrong." (C3 → C5 shortcut).
    * *Success:* "I feel this is wrong (C3), but I recognize there is no C1 violation, therefore this is a C5 convention based on shared values."

## 4. Summary for Collaborators
The C3 Diagnostic tool doesn't try to measure the "soul" or the sensation itself. It simply **audits the chain of anchors**. 

If the anchor chain from C5 (The Rule) to C1 (The Physics) is broken and only goes through C3 (The Feeling), the argument is flagged as **Subjective/Non-Knowledge**.
