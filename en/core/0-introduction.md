# Part 0 — Why This Exists

## 0.1 The Central Problem
The words "reality," "truth," and "knowledge" are currently used to refer to fundamentally different concepts without any distinction. This semantic confusion is not merely an academic issue; it has severe material consequences in fields such as medicine, engineering, politics, and the management of global conflicts.

The goal of this framework is to separate meanings that should never have been merged. When ontological categories are confused, catastrophic decisions are made on foundations that cannot support them.

## 0.2 The Method: Reverse Engineering
This framework does not begin with an abstract definition of truth. Its method is **reverse engineering** from technological success. We start from an undeniable material fact to reconstruct definitions in a way that is consistent with what we already know works.

## 0.3 The Foundational Axiom
**"You are reading this text on an electronic device that works."**

This fact is not a hypothesis; it is the material condition that allows this information to reach you. For this device to operate, thousands of scientific principles and physical laws must be true simultaneously with extreme precision.

### The Counterfactual Argument
If it were even possible, in principle, for any of the scientific foundations upon which this technology is based to be false, the technology could never have functioned. Airplanes do not fly by consensus, nor do pacemakers sustain life through cultural narrative; their operation is the material proof of an alignment with reality that is independent of the observer's belief.

## 0.4 Universal Behavior Argument
It is an observable fact that individuals from all cultures, ideologies, and religions adopt and use the same technology. A mobile device operates on the same principles in the hands of a physicist as it does in the hands of someone who denies science.

Words and narratives can sustain any worldview (C4/C5), but human behavior—by entrusting one's life to a technological infrastructure—reveals the criterion of reality (C1/C2) that we all implicitly accept.
