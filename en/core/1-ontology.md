# Part 1 — Layered Ontology

The first step of the framework is to establish a map of the territory that distinguishes qualitatively different types of existence. Reality is not a single amorphous mass; it has a hierarchical structure based on its independence from the observer.

## 1.1 The Reality Layers (C0 - C5)

We define six layers ranging from absolute physics to pure institutions:

| Layer | Name | Operational Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C0** | **RAW Reality** | The totality of what exists, including what we do not yet know exists (e.g., the electromagnetic spectrum before discovery). |
| **C1** | **Measurable Reality** | Physically detectable through instruments. The domain of resistance: a bullet kills even if you don't see it. |
| **C2** | **Perception** | How the nervous system processes C1. A "controlled hallucination" corrected by the resistance of reality. |
| **C3** | **Expression** | The public bridge of C2. Includes art and incipient language: how we represent or name a sensation. |
| **C4** | **Organic Conventions** | Languages, myths, and beliefs that emerge spontaneously and collectively. They have no single "author." |
| **C5** | **Formal Institutions** | Deliberate laws, ideologies, and systems. They require infrastructure (police, bureaucracy) to be sustained. |

## 1.2 Hierarchy and Asymmetry
Layers are not watertight compartments, but their relationship is asymmetrical. The influence of lower layers on higher ones is qualitatively greater and more deterministic:
* **Downward Influence (C5 -> C2):** A law (C5) can ban a drug, but it cannot change the drug's chemical reaction within the body (C1/C2).
* **Upward Influence (C2 -> C5):** Five beers (C2) modify your cognition and moral judgment (C4/C5) in minutes. Physical reality always reclaims its hierarchy.

## 1.3 The Law of Epistemic Friction
The further the distance from C0, the more energy is required to maintain the narrative.
* **C0/C1** requires no energy to persist: gravity works without anyone believing in it.
* **C5** requires constant energy: laws need courts and prisons. If collective effort ceases, the fiction vanishes (e.g., money, borders).

## 1.4 Persistence Mechanisms
Why do fictions (C5) persist if they are unanchored from reality?
1. **Historical Inertia:** Momentum generated by past energy investment (e.g., the persistence of religious institutions).
2. **Active Defense:** The effort of individuals or groups who are direct beneficiaries of the current state of conventions.
3. **Hardwired Biases:** Narratives that feel "natural" because they exploit C0 evolutionary mechanisms not yet mapped in C1.
