# Part 2 — Operational Epistemology

If Ontology (Part 1) is the map, Operational Epistemology is the compass. In this system, we do not treat knowledge as something contemplative, but as something **operative**: something is "true" if we can build upon it without the system collapsing.

## 2.1 Unification of Truth and Knowledge
For the purposes of this framework, **Truth and Knowledge are functionally the same concept**.
* "Truth" is often used as an unreachable absolute.
* "Knowledge" is often used as a subjective possession.

Here, we unify them under the definition of **Verified Intersubjective Reality**. If a claim has passed the validation pipeline and allows for successful predictions in C1/C2, we treat it as functional knowledge.

## 2.2 Precision Vocabulary
To avoid semantic inflation, we distinguish three terms that natural language often confuses:

1.  **Knowledge:** Information that has passed the Validation Pipeline and is anchored to reality (C1/C2).
2.  **Correct:** Internal consistency within a rule system. Something can be "correct" within a fiction (C5) but not be "knowledge" of reality (e.g., the laws of magic in a book or an arbitrary accounting regulation).
3.  **Certainty:** A subjective neurochemical sensation (C2/C3). Certainty is not an indicator of truth; it is a mental state that can be completely unanchored from reality.

## 2.3 The Validation Pipeline (8 Filters)
Every claim seeking the status of "Knowledge" must undergo this sequential flow. If it fails a step, it is discarded or labeled as "Fiction" or "Indefinite."

1.  **Existence Filter (C0/C1):** Does the referent of the claim have a material basis or is it a dangling pointer?
2.  **Measurability Filter:** Is it possible to design an instrument or experiment to detect this phenomenon?
3.  **Strong Intersubjectivity Filter:** Can observers with different ideologies replicate the same data?
4.  **Non-Contradiction Filter:** Is it consistent with already proven technological success (Foundational Axiom)?
5.  **Global Coherence Filter:** Does it fit within the general map of sciences without requiring "magical" exceptions?
6.  **Parsimony Filter (Ockham's Razor):** Is it the simplest explanation that does not omit data?
7.  **Falsifiability Filter:** Does a condition exist under which this claim would be considered false?
8.  **Fertility Filter (Progressivity):** Does the answer generate more precise questions, or does it close off thought?

## 2.4 Progressivity Criterion
An idea is valid not only for its current precision but for its capacity to be refined. "Dead" knowledge (dogma) stops. Functional knowledge (C2) is always a frontier expanding toward C0.

> **Golden Rule:** If a claim requires you to ignore how your mobile phone works in order to be true, the claim is false.
